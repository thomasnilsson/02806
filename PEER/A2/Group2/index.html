Part 1: Telling Data Stories

What is the Oxford English Dictionary's defintion of a narrative?

The oxford english dictionary defines a narrative as:
“An account of a series of events, facts, etc., given in order and with the establishing of connections between them.”
Essentially, it is a representation of a story where the representation is the order of which the events are related.

What is your favorite visualization among the examples in section 3? Explain why in a few words.

I enjoyed the visualization of Budget Forecasts, Compared With Reality by the New York Times. First of all, the visualization guides the observer through the narrative using six slides. Each slide represents an important point for the narrative, and keeps the observer interested. The graph is kept simple, and very consistent in style and coloring. Compared to the other visualizations in the paper, it represents a manageable amount of data, and hides the remaining data for the user to explore. Finally, the use of annotations is kept to a bare minimum, but the ones provided helps explaining key points in the timeline.

What's the point of Figure 7?

Figure 7 presents an analysis of the design space of narrative visualization. This is useful for identifying recurring elements in different visualizations, and categorizing these design features. The figure gives a complete overview of the genres, visual narrative and narrative structures of all analysed visualizations. From the figure, a clear distinction between each visualization can be made using these different patterns.

The most frequent design choices to create a visual narrative by the category (Visual Structuring, Highlighting and Transition Guidance) are;

The most frequent design choices to create a narrative structure by category (Ordering, Interactivity and Messaging) are;

Part 2: Visualizing reported murders in New York City

The purpose of this section is to visualize reported murders in New York City from 2006 to 2016, both geographically and temporally. Two datasets were used:

  • a dataset specifying the global coordinates of each of the five boroughs and their features, and
  • a dataset specifying each reported murder for the period, including the date of the report, the hour of the day at which the complaint was submitted, and the geographical location at which the murder allegedly took place.

The visualization is showcased in figure 1. You are invited to interact with the visualization in the following ways:

  • Click and drag in the timeline to visualize murders for a subset of the period. The vertical scale of the bar chart will update in accordance with the width of your current selection.
  • Click the timeline outside the selection (marked gray if present) to reset the selection to the entire period.
  • Click the 'Animate' button to quickly visualize how the distribution of murders-- both in terms of location and time of day-- developed over the entire period. This part of the visualization implies a decrease in the total number of murders with roughly the same daily distribution (please see the bar chart on the right).
